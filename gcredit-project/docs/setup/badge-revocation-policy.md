# Badge Revocation Policy

**Effective Date**: January 1, 2026  
**Version**: 1.0

## Purpose

This policy defines the circumstances under which digital badges may be revoked and the process for badge holders to appeal revocation decisions.

## Scope

This policy applies to all digital badges issued through the G-Credit Badge Management System to employees, contractors, and affiliates.

## Grounds for Revocation

Badges may be revoked under the following circumstances:

### 1. Fraudulent Claims
- Badge was obtained through falsified documentation or misrepresentation
- Evidence of cheating or unauthorized assistance in assessments
- Use of someone else's credentials or work to claim a badge

### 2. Policy Violations
- Violation of organizational code of conduct
- Breach of confidentiality or security policies
- Misuse of badge credentials for unauthorized purposes

### 3. Expired Competency
- Skills or knowledge no longer current (for time-limited badges)
- Failure to maintain required certifications or licenses
- Non-compliance with continuing education requirements

### 4. Administrative Errors
- Badge issued in error to incorrect recipient
- Duplicate badges issued for the same achievement
- Technical errors in badge issuance process

### 5. Employment Status Changes
- Termination of employment for cause
- Departure from organization (for internal-only badges)
- Change in role that no longer requires the credential

## Revocation Process

### Notification
1. Badge holder will receive email notification of pending revocation
2. Notification includes:
   - Reason for revocation
   - Effective date (minimum 5 business days notice)
   - Appeal process instructions
   - Contact information for questions

### Documentation
- All revocation decisions must be documented
- Supporting evidence retained for audit purposes
- Revocation reason visible in badge metadata

### System Actions
- Badge status changed to "REVOKED" in system
- Badge removed from public verification
- Verification URLs return revocation notice
- Badge holder notified via email

## Appeal Process

Badge holders have the right to appeal a revocation decision:

### Step 1: Submit Appeal
- Email: g-credit@outlook.com
- Subject: "Badge Revocation Appeal - [Badge Name]"
- Include: Badge ID, reason for appeal, supporting evidence

### Step 2: Review
- Appeals reviewed by Badge Governance Committee
- Review completed within 10 business days
- Decision communicated via email

### Step 3: Final Decision
- Committee decision is final
- If appeal successful: Badge reinstated with notation
- If appeal denied: Badge remains revoked

## Rights and Responsibilities

### Badge Holders
- Maintain accuracy of information associated with badges
- Report any errors or concerns about badge credentials
- Comply with badge usage guidelines
- Protect badge verification URLs from misuse

### Issuers
- Only issue badges to eligible recipients
- Maintain accurate records of issuance criteria
- Review revocation requests promptly
- Provide clear communication to badge holders

### Organization
- Maintain transparent revocation policies
- Provide fair appeal process
- Protect personal information of badge holders
- Ensure consistent application of policy

## Reinstatement

Revoked badges may be reinstated if:

1. **Appeal Successful**: Committee determines revocation was in error
2. **Corrective Action**: Badge holder completes required remediation
3. **Time-Based**: For temporary revocations, after specified period
4. **Administrative**: If revocation was due to system error

Reinstated badges include notation of revocation history in metadata.

## Data Retention

- Revoked badge records retained for 7 years
- Supporting documentation archived per data retention policy
- Personal information handled per privacy policy
- Public verification pages display "Badge Revoked" message

## Policy Review

This policy is reviewed annually and updated as needed. Changes communicated to all badge holders via email and system notifications.
